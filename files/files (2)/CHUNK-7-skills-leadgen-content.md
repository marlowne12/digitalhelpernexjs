# Outreach Sequence Skill

## Purpose
Generate personalized multi-touch outreach sequences for lead nurturing.

## Standard 5-Touch Sequence

### Touch 1: Initial Outreach (Day 1)
- **Type:** Email
- **Goal:** Pattern interrupt, establish relevance
- **Length:** 50-100 words
- **Must include:** Specific observation about their business

### Touch 2: Value Add (Day 3)
- **Type:** Email
- **Goal:** Provide immediate value, no ask
- **Length:** 75-125 words
- **Must include:** Quick tip or resource relevant to their pain point

### Touch 3: Social Proof (Day 7)
- **Type:** Email
- **Goal:** Build credibility
- **Length:** 75-100 words
- **Must include:** Brief result from similar business

### Touch 4: Direct Ask (Day 10)
- **Type:** Email
- **Goal:** Clear CTA for conversation
- **Length:** 50-75 words
- **Must include:** Specific, low-commitment ask

### Touch 5: Breakup (Day 14)
- **Type:** Email
- **Goal:** Final attempt, leave door open
- **Length:** 50-75 words
- **Must include:** Permission to close the loop

## Email Templates

### Touch 1 - GMB Focus
```
Subject: [Business Name] on Google Maps

Hi [Name],

I was looking for [service type] in [City] and came across [Business Name]. I noticed your Google Business Profile [specific observation - few photos/low review count/no recent posts].

Here's the thing - [X]% of local searches lead to a call or visit within 24 hours, but only for businesses that show up correctly.

Would a quick GMB audit be useful? Takes me 10 minutes to review, and I'll share exactly what's holding you back.

[Signature]
```

### Touch 1 - Website Focus
```
Subject: [Business Name] website thought

Hi [Name],

Just visited [Business Name]'s website while researching [industry] companies in [City].

[Specific observation - mobile issues, slow load, no clear CTA]. Given that [X%] of your potential customers are likely searching on their phones, this could be costing you leads.

Happy to do a quick 5-minute video walkthrough of what I'd fix first if you're interested.

[Signature]
```

### Touch 2 - Value Add
```
Subject: Quick tip for [Business Name]

Hi [Name],

Wanted to share something quick that might help.

[Specific actionable tip based on their situation - e.g., "Adding your service area to your GMB description can boost visibility for 'near me' searches."]

This alone can improve your visibility for "[relevant local keyword]" searches.

Let me know if you want me to flag any other quick wins I spotted.

[Signature]
```

### Touch 3 - Social Proof
```
Subject: How [Similar Business Type] in [Nearby City] got more calls

Hi [Name],

Quick story - we worked with a [similar business type] who was getting buried in local search.

After [specific change], they saw [specific result - X% more calls, Y new reviews, etc.] in [timeframe].

Their situation looked a lot like yours. Happy to share what we did if you're curious.

[Signature]
```

### Touch 4 - Direct Ask
```
Subject: 15 minutes, [Name]?

Hi [Name],

I'll keep this short - I think I can help [Business Name] get more [calls/leads/visibility] from local search.

Do you have 15 minutes this week for a quick call? I'll share 2-3 specific things you could improve, no strings attached.

[Calendar link or "Just reply with a time that works"]

[Signature]
```

### Touch 5 - Breakup
```
Subject: Should I close your file?

Hi [Name],

I've reached out a few times about helping [Business Name] with [local search/GMB/website] but haven't heard back.

Totally understand if the timing isn't right. I'll assume you're all set for now and won't keep bothering you.

If things change down the road, I'm always happy to help. Just reply to this email.

Best of luck with [Business Name].

[Signature]
```

## Subject Line Rules

**Do:**
- Include business name when possible
- Keep under 40 characters
- Make it look 1:1, not bulk
- Use lowercase (except proper nouns)

**Don't:**
- Use "Quick question" (overused)
- ALL CAPS
- Spam triggers (Free, Act Now, Limited Time)
- Misleading claims

## Personalization Requirements

Before writing any sequence, gather:
1. Business name (spelled correctly)
2. Owner/decision maker first name
3. One specific observation about their online presence
4. How long they've been in business (if findable)
5. Any recent news or updates about them

# Local SEO Content Brief Skill

## Purpose
Create content briefs optimized for local search and AI search engines.

## Brief Components

### 1. Content Metadata
- Target keyword (primary)
- Secondary keywords (3-5)
- Search intent (informational/commercial/transactional)
- Target word count
- Content type (blog/service page/location page)
- Target location

### 2. SERP Analysis
- Top 3 ranking pages (title, word count, approach)
- Content gaps (what they don't cover)
- Unique angle for our content

### 3. Outline Structure

```
# H1: [Primary Keyword + Location + Value]

## H2: [Problem/Need Statement]
- Introduce the problem in local context
- 100-150 words

## H2: [Solution Overview]
- Connect service to local need
- 150-200 words

## H2: [Key Benefit 1]
### H3: Supporting point
### H3: Local example or case study

## H2: [Key Benefit 2]
### H3: Supporting point
### H3: Local proof point

## H2: [How It Works / Process]
### H3: Step 1
### H3: Step 2
### H3: Step 3

## H2: Why Choose [Business] in [Location]
- Local credibility
- Years in business
- Team expertise
- 150 words

## H2: Service Areas
- Brief mention of areas served
- Can include nearby cities

## H2: Frequently Asked Questions
- 5-7 questions
- Natural, conversational phrasing
- 50-100 word answers each

## H2: [CTA Section]
- Clear next step
- Local contact info
```

### 4. FAQ Requirements
- Source from Google "People Also Ask"
- Include questions actual customers ask
- Phrase conversationally
- Answers should be quotable by AI

### 5. Local Optimization
- NAP placement (name, address, phone)
- Local schema markup notes
- Geo-modified keyword placements
- Local trust signals to include

### 6. AI Search Optimization
- Clear, factual statements
- "X is..." and "X means..." constructions
- Specific numbers/statistics
- Quotable FAQ answers
- No fluff paragraphs
